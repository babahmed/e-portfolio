Practical activity: Article Questions

Question 1: What are the main risks identified by Authors Verner et al (2014) and Anton & Nucu (2020) in their articles:

The main risks that Verner J.M (2014) identified in his work on Risks and risk mitigation in global software development: A tertiary study are :

Global software development risk (GSD): The primary goal of the GSD is to ensure improvements in market efficiency, obtain access to a larger number of resources at lower cost, and achieve and maintain competitive objectives in the absence of these goals, the GDS can be at risk when;

- Organizations start shifting all or parts of software development to another country with a meagre cost for destinations and to countries where the expected skills are more readily obtainable.

- Multinational organizations begin to distribute their software development activities across numerous lesser sites, many of which are in different countries, for example; Bosch and Phillips. Here, the global subsidiary that needs the software can be viewed as the client and the subsidiaries that develop the software can be considered equal to software dealers.

The main risks that Anton & Nucu, (2020) identified in their work on Enterprise risk management are:

- Low earning performances in enterprises or firms

- Probability of financial distress and associated cost.

Which of the frameworks discussed in the Unit 3 lecture cast would you use to capture and categorise the risks?

The main risks identified by author Verner J.M (2014) in his work on Risk management and risk mitigation in global software development can be categorized under ;

- The Information Security Management system(ISMS): This framework captures the Global software development risk as likened to an information risk that could be managed by the ISMS if there is proper governance and contribution from senior management and major heads of a company, with all ethical standards and certifications of this software being known.

The main risks identified by Anton & Nuru,(2020) in their work on Enterprises risk management can be categorized under;

- The PDCA (Plan, Do, Check and Act) framework: This framework was created to capture the risks that Enterprise managements fall into.

Add a risk and suggested mitigation to the module forum.

The Global Software Development(GSD) risk identified by Verner et Al (2014) can be mitigated with the help of a Software Engineering project manager (SEPM) using Hoffman et al,(2016) risk management system that consists of 5 major parts;

- Strategy system; that supplies tasks and objectives - Intellectual resources system; Using people's efforts and behaviours. - Technology system; Using materials and technical equipment. - Structure system; using formal structures and procedures. - Management System; That supports, monitors and drives the attainment of goals

The risk of Low earning performance and the Probability of financial distress and associated cost that was identified by authors Anton & Nucu,(2020) can be mitigated through using the PDCA mandate which stipulated 4 phases to following in mitigating Enterprise risks :

- Plan phase; Creating a plan. - Do Phase; Putting the plan into action; Like communicating with department heads to capture their risk appetite ratings and collating team members in other to review and analyze the estimation of all that has been gathered and then adopting new procedures, new responsibilities, hiring new or creating new roles. - Check phase; Checking that the actions are done properly - Act phase; Acting according to the plans, modifying the procedures, roles and responsibilities done in the Do phase.
