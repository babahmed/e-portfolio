Initial Post :- Collaborative Discussion 1: Project Failures Study

What do you believe are the three most common reasons for project failure?.

1. Poor communication and incompetent project manager.

2. Inadequate organization support and task definitions.

3. Lack of adequate knowledge.

“Give two examples of failures that support your choices (there are several examples in the lecturecast).”

An important case study is the issue of a medium-sized international software product company. The company releases new software versions regularly and its products can be characterized as complex model-based software. The failure of the case was that blocker-type defects of the program are often detected after the product releases (Lehtinen et al, 2014). This project's failure is due to the lack of adequate skills required and knowledge in testing and having code quality control in the case of adding new codes to the old ones. Developers and testers should identify this common error during the development and testing phase, but they didn't.

Another Case Study is a medium-sized international software product company with approximately 100 employees. The product is delivered for the customers through installation projects that occasionally include the development of new features. New software versions are released regularly. The failure selected for analysis in the case was that the installation projects are too challenging to be performed efficiently. This project failure was due to lack of communication and incompetent project managers who has failed to develop a method of creating checklists and simplifying the installation process which could have minimize the failure.

Initial Post :- Collaborative Discussion 2: Factors affecting user experience

The expression of human experience to a phenomenon often tends to be subject and relative and over time changes with much familiarity with the concept or object. Kujala, S. (2011) examined that the definition of user experience is lacking on a generally acceptable ground due to its multidimensional nature. The human experience is embodied in emotions and this emotion, speaks of our judgemental stance on the experience we have had.

This emotion, replete itself in the experience and interaction with technology. From our visual aspect, we often give judgements that changes in the course of a continuous experience of the technology. As Minge and Thurge (2018) noted, there are two products relation emotion which can further be subdivided into the; initial and later stages.

The first stage talks about the visual appeal of the product as it presents itself to us while the second stage speaks of the Influence associated with the constant use of the product. Herein, it moves from a stage of visual appeal to a detailed experience. Minge and Thurge (2018) call this the; hedonic halo effect(beautiful is usable) and the pragmatic halo effect(usable is beautiful).

As a suggestion to build user experience and interface a product, the development of UI basically will develop the user experience and this includes layout, design, and prototyping. Prototype links all product screens together and allows users to explore products. It is used to showcase products to stakeholders, understand developers, and collect feedback from potential users.

Kujala, S, & Roto, V, & Vaananen-Vainio-Mattila, K, & Karapanos, E, & Sinnela 2011, 'UX Curve: A method for evaluating long-term user experience, Interacting with Computers 23 (2011) 473–483

Minge, M, & Thuring, M 2018, 'Hedonic and pragmatic halo effects at early stages of User Experience', International Journal of Human-Computer Studies 109: 13-25.

Collaborative Discussion 2: Factors affecting user experience Response to Yvon https://www.my-course.co.uk/mod/hsuforum/discuss.php?d=319713

The term user experience has gained much popularity these days. However, regarding its definition and scope, there is still no definite answer. Law et. al. (2009) has conducted a survey on 275 researchers and practitioners. Authors recommended the term to be scoped to products, systems, services, and objects that a person interacts with through a user interface.

As Minge and Thuring (2018) pointed out, there are hedonic halo effect ('beautiful is usable') and a pragmatic halo effect ('usable gets beautiful) during interaction with a product. The CUE model in figure 1 tried to summarize the components of user experiences into three categories, namely perception of non-instrumental qualities, emotional reactions, and perception of instrumental qualities. In my opinion, this is generally true. However, it is worth noting that the perception of instrumental qualities is very subjective. A system may be useful to a group of users but not the others as they may have different needs.

Therefore, I would suggest to add Persona to the components of user experience. People of similar background and traits may have more similar reactions towards the interaction with the interface.
