Unit 7 - Data Types

In this module, I learnt importance of the differences between qualitative and quantitative data and the potential uses of each type of data before collecting any information and how it helps to ensure that the data collected is appropriate for the research objectives and can be effectively analyzed and presented. Quantitative data can be used for descriptive and inferential statistics, but it is important to first consider processes such as data cleansing and validation. Reflecting on these processes before collecting data can help to ensure the accuracy and reliability of the results.

Initial Post: Collaborative discussion

Abi who is a statistical programmer and also a researcher was given a project from a manufacturer to review the nutritional value of cereal, Whizzz. From the data collected, Abi found out that it refutes the claim that Whizzz is nutritional and could even be harmful. The dilemma for Abi thus lies in the action to take; contravening the data to produce a favourable side for Whizzz since his report could produce both outcomes.

From the above dynamics, it is important to note that ethics is a prerequisite in all professional discipline and conduct as it ensures the normative order of doing things to avert negative consequences (F., Vanclay et al, 2013). The paramount obligation of every society is to safety, health, property and welfare of the public (Holemans & Lodewyckx 1996). Thus, professionals like Abi are expected to give fair and proper conduct of their research in terms of the appropriate and valid analytical methods as well as avoiding a conflict of interest, and risk, but rather showing competence (Jamal & Bowie, 1995).

According to the UK Research Integrity Office Code of Practice for Research (2022), researchers are advised that they can report to the relevant authority if they suspect the misinterpretation or misuse of the result. In this way, Abi will be doing the public more good because, from his analysis of the nutritional value of Whizzz, his findings contradict the manufacturer's claim. Failure to do so will be a breach of the BCS Code of Conduct (2022) which opines that the public interest must be protected as a professional.

F., Vanclay et al, (2013), note that part of the ethical conduct of research holds on to the principle of reliability and validity which must promote the objectiveness of the research analysis and findings. To that effect, the key legislations in the UK such as the Food Standards Act 1999 and Food Safety Act 1990 (Food Standards Agency, 2022), can be applied here. Abi is expected to produce both sides of the results to his employer and when they go against this result to release the one with a positive remark without a check and solutions to the negative effects will result in a violation of the UK law safeguarding consumers' health.

The Cambridge Analytica was charged to court for carrying out illegal use of surveys through the use of the internet. The ethical issue in this case study is the inadequate regulation of private user data. The participants in the survey were not aware of the use of the survey for political or commercial purposes though the company committed an ethical offense of deception, disregarding consent as a research ethics.

Conclusively, it is important to note that our moral obligation requires us to do the right thing even if no one is watching. The best of conduct for Abi is to act for the greater good. Hence, it requires him to act within the ethics of his profession by doing the expectancies above.
