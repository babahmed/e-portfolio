Unit 1 - deation | Purpose

In this unit, I learnt that as a researcher, it is essential to understand why we carry out research. The three main reasons are to explore a topic or situation, to describe our findings, and to explain why something is the way it is. One of the key approaches in research is the scientific method, which involves observation, hypothesis, reasoning, and testing. We also learnt the types of reasoning used in the scientific method, namely deductive and inductive reasoning. Both forms of reasoning are ways of reaching a conclusion based on evidence and reasoning, but they differ in the direction they take us. Deduction moves from an idea to an observation, while induction moves from an observation to an idea. Deduction can lead to a perfect conclusion if all assumptions are correct, but this can be difficult to achieve in a non-lab setting. Induction is more frequently used to solve everyday problems, but the conclusions may not always be right. Before conducting any research, it is important to consider the ethics of the research and ensure that any potential participants are treated fairly and with respect.

Initial Post

Every discipline requires ethics for its regulation and functionality and thus, computing society is not exempted. The goal in this setting, is to consciously affect the performance and functionality of a computer system, which in turn directly affects the well-being of system users and does not directly affect others, and this can only be done by professional ethics (Loui, M. C. & Miller, K. W. 2008).

To that end, in my observation of the ACM code of ethics and professional conduct, I selected the case study of the U.S children's internet protection act (CIPA). This area looks into a mandate for public schools and libraries to block inappropriate content that will be harmful to minors. This can be carried out using the 'blocker plus.' The problem thus lies in a review which claimed that content is being blocked inappropriately (ACM 2022).

A careful review of content being blocked inappropriately showed that the feedback mechanism that feeds information to the machine has been compromised. It showed clearly that content related to climate, vaccination, and gand ay, lesbian marriage was blocked by the CIPA (ACM 2022). This led to the suppression of legitimate topics of public interest leading to a violation of the BCS code of conduct on 1.2 and 1.4 as well as 1. C (BCS 2015). Also, an observation into the BCS code of conduct for 3d and 3e & ACM principles 1.3, 2.4 and 2.7 shows that blocker plus did not provide enough information to users and stakeholders on its usage. Excellence is perhaps the most important task of a professional. Computer professionals must strive for quality and be aware of the serious negative consequences that can result from poor quality in a system (Loui, M. C. & Miller, K. W. 2008).

Furthermore, Blocker Plus's decision to include a machine learning model has been challenged for violating ACM Principle 2.5, which requires due diligence in the application of machine learning techniques (ACM, 2022).

In conclusion, research by (Llanso, 2020) has shown that the use of a machine as in the case of the CIPA and the use of blocker plus, is vital to flitter out unnecessary information. However, this must be done within the ethical code of conduct and the respect for other's rights. From the research so far, it was quite interesting to know that where one's right ends, another begin. Thus, the reason for the conflict of rights.
