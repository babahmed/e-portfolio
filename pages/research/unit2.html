Unit 2 - Ethics | Conducts | Ideation

Here, we learnt that defining the topic of our research is a crucial step in ensuring success. We start with an initial research idea and then ask relevant questions to refine it before preparing a research proposal, which is the foundation of our research. We were introduced to an important element of the proposal which is the literature review, a survey of scholarly sources on a specific topic. It provides an overview of current knowledge and helps us identify relevant theories, methods, and gaps in the existing research. We learnt the process of completing a literature review and how to present it. We also learnt about formulating and revising research questions and the different parts of a research proposal and explore methods for formulating a research idea, identify ways of transforming research ideas into crafted research questions and proposals, conduct a literature search, critique the literature, and present a literature review.

My Response to : Mohamed Danialou https://www.my-course.co.uk/mod/hsuforum/discuss.php?d=326157

Hello Mohamed,

I commend your critical approach to the case study on abusive workplace behaviour. Abusive behaviour in the workplace, also known as bullying, is increasingly being recognized as a serious problem in our society today. This is a problem that comes with aggressive behaviour especial from a superior who acts with much influence of power (Cowie, H. et al, 2002). As exemplified in the case study of Diana who experienced abuse in her workplace by Max, and the response from Jean the team manager, shows the high level of unethical behaviour within their workplace.

In your explanation, you referred to the (Equality Act, 2010) & (UK Government, 2011) on the need to seek protection for employees and workers in an abusive workplace. This shows that the law is total against abuse at the workplace and seeks to protect the affected. Thus concerning the BCS 2022 code of ethics, Max's abusive behaviour violated several principles. BCS 2022 principles 1.1, 1.3, and 1.4, seeks respect for the well-being of others as well as against gender discrimination, which was violated by Max. Also, Jean's response to Diana showed a violation of principle 3.3 which holds that "members accept professional responsibility for your work and for the work of colleagues who are defined in a given context as working under your supervision" (BCS 2022). This was on the ground that she acted unprofessional whereas, she was expected to critically examine the situation and condemn her action of Max.

Einarsen and Skogstad (1996) argue that a person is bullied when they are repeatedly exposed to negative actions at work. Adding, he said that to be a victim of such bullying, one must also feel inferior when in a current situation that is maintained. This was clearly shown in the case of Diana whose confidence was further affected by the response from Jean. It is on this note that efficiency and safety at the workplace can be guaranteed when the principles and code of conduct are duly observed.
