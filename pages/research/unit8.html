Unit 8 - Data Testing

In week 8, the focus was on analyzing quantitative data using various methods. This included looking at ways to describe the data and how to perform hypothesis tests, also known as statistical inference. This process involves using probability to quantify the uncertainty of inferences made about a population from a sample of data. The data used in this analysis were organized into datasets, with each row representing an individual observation and each column representing a variable of interest. It was important to use appropriate methodology for the level of measurement of each variable in order to extract meaningful and useful business intelligence from the data. One approach to summarizing the data was to construct graphical summaries, while another approach was to use summary measures such as measures of location and dispersion. Hypothesis testing was also discussed as a way to provide evidence about assumptions regarding population values. Overall, the focus of week 8 was on using quantitative data to make inferences about populations and test assumptions.

Summary Post Collaborative discussion

From the collaborative discussion group on the assessment of Abi who is a statistician and was given the job to review the nutritional value of cereal, Whzizz, results from the review showed a contradiction to the claim that Whizzz is nutritional. However, Abi being a professional could contravene this data to produce a positive result. The moral and legal worthiness of his actions becomes the basis of this examination.

I concluded following the above dynamics that, it will be unprofessional for Abi to produce contradicting data which will more likely be harmful to the public. As stated in the BCS Code of Conduct (2022), the public interest must be protected at all costs. Hence, Abi is expected to report to the relevant authorities following the UK Research Integrity Office Code of Practice for Research (2022), where researchers are advised that they can report to the relevant authority if they suspect the misinterpretation or misuse of the result.

This thus concludes that our moral obligation requires us to do the right thing even if no one is watching. The best of conduct for Abi is to act for the greater good. Hence, it requires him to act within the ethics of his profession by doing the expectancies above.
