REFLECTION ON MODULE

Introduction

Object-Oriented Programming spanning from November 2021 has been the most practical of the modules encountered so far. It begins by easing you into what to expect in future modules starting from the easily graspable concepts into the much tougher implementations. It started by introducing me to the basics of systems and the possible impacts and implications of a system failure. This gives you an idea of how important it is to build a system that works and need to cover all possible edge cases. It latter introduced the basic concepts of OOP and the coding challenges gradually takes care of introducing and explaining most of the concepts of solid principles.

Initial Post

The initial post further explores scenarios where system failure has extended effect on all dependencies, and I was able to understand the impact of possible system failure on the society at large and not just the organization involved. A review at other peer contributions gives insight into how systems were restored after a failure and approaches taken to prevent further failure in response to the facilitators comment.

Objects and Classes

Even with my developer background, understanding object-oriented programing theoretically can sometimes be a challenge. I use these concepts at work but in most cases do not know what each concept is called and some of its advantages over each the other. One of the major challenges in everyday design was knowing when to choose composition over inheritance and vice-versa. I do face these challenges most time in software development, but o was able to look at it from a scholarly perception taking into consideration the advantage of one over the other in a theoretical approach.

Excercises (CODING)

Coming from a C#/Java background, I must confess that that one of the major turnoffs for me in python is the indentation but with this section, I was forced to break through the barrier of learning the indentation typed programing of python. Codio was also a relatively easy platform to use breaking the exercises into pieces to make it less overwhelming for absolute beginners to understand the concept of python then ultimately OOP from the python implementation. The basics were all covered, and you basically get experienced at declaring classes, functions and methods.

Databases

I have quite a handful of experience generally with SQL (MSSQL, POSTGRES and MYSQL) and as such, this module was like a refresher for me. It is however important to mention that I was forced to look into the core concepts of document-based SQL such as mongo DB in other to understand the compact behind this new data persistence method. In that, I know the difference between these two approaches and how they scale differently including what the industry is doing to bridge the Gap with modern concepts such as NewSQL.

Hospital Management

This module gave me the vibe of a solutions architect. Having to design a system based on requirement, map out the components, classes and the relationships. Determine how the implementation will be and stating the entry point into each module is like being in control of all variables. Choosing and applying the concepts thought was very challenging as for someone just learning python, its easier to get carried away with procedural implementations. Quite often, I find myself refactoring my code properly after going far down the procedural way them moving function back into the object that they should belong to properly achieve proper OOP.

Bringing it together

This module gave me a broader look into how decisions are made in class components and relationship. Being a software engineer, I get to explore more of the daily concept I use with detailed and foundational understanding of the underlying concepts that births some of the decisions we make and approach to system implementation. The part on Normalization explains why we flatten a class into related bits linked by primary and foreign key constraints. I also went further to study the different types of normalizations that exists in the field. Although, I did not introduce access to the database in my final project for the hospital management opting for the in-memory approach instead, normalization section gave me a pretty good idea of what my data structure was going to look like if I choose to create my schema and introduce a persistent layer.

Conclusion

This module basically sets the pace for any aspiring developer to thrive on, it is some of the missing link that affects some self-taught developers as these basic foundations are missing in their journey to understanding software development and programing frameworks. I am glad to be part of this module as this would greatly impact my understanding of the basic concepts I use in my implementations.
