REFLECTION ON MODULE

The general discussion in unit one was the introduction to network and information security with an interactive approach using problem solving by reviewing the topic on medical mannequin. Right from the beginning of this module, the lecturer was very interactive and approachable, and it was much easier for me to attend the seminars being that most were scheduled for Saturday. I was paired with Sathira and Yin with focus site “allmytypes.co.uk” and these are arguably some of the easiest people to work with. This topic shows how digitization in every sector (including the medical sector) comes with its risk and requirements. We got introduced to some basic vulnerabilities on devices and software such as the brute force and DDOS attack. These terms I am familiar with, but interactions went further to explain how they are applicable to systems and software. One major takeaway from this unit and the unit 2 is the need for using strong longer dynamic password. Also, in my opinion, I believer DDOS can only be managed and not completely eradicated. Only a mechanism that reacts promptly to such attack can be put in place as argued also by Sathira.

In Unit 3 presented an interesting background into how network works with the different network layer model explained including TCP/IP, ISO/OSI7 and the network tools such as ping, trace route, netstat, whois, dig and nslookup. I am quite familiar with these terms but got a refresher on how they work and their different use and how ping can be used as a form of attack.

In Unit 4, we went practical with our tools and saw their effect. The results were discussed with the team, and we basically came with similar results with that of other teammates. This is likely because the target sites are hosted in similar environments. However, Thien and Yin pointed to the location for both results being different despite having the same host. This is something to investigate off this module. We can pick this up in group discussions

In Unit 5, things got a little serious with the introduction of more network tools for packet diagnostics such as wireshark and vulnerability scanners such as Nmap, KaliLinux. Understanding what they do is one thing, using them is another. Quite a bunch of how-to videos were required to get basic understanding of their usage.

We were tasked with presenting a proposal for penetration test on our allocated website. Initially we started some of the works and provided the results in our proposal, but we were promptly corrected by our tutor on this initial draft being our proposal and recommendation for a penetration test. There was also emphasis on governing bodies on information security such as GDPR and PCIDSS. I believe we will discuss more about these in latter modules.

Unit 7 went deeper into the risks in information security compromise and some of the guidelines that are applicable to these risks. One of the discussions that came up was how GDPR is not applicable in the United States but has a similar version called the CCPA which covers more of the household data. This was new to me and got me curious looking into what is applicable to the US and its local equivalent in Nigeria.

Unit 8, we reviewed a case study on data breach, and I choose to investigate the Dublin Mint Office Limited. Using the tutor’s advice, I was specific about the GDPR article the case contradicts. The cost of this contradiction and the possible mitigation. Basically, there is always a cost implication to these risk factors which are always more expensive than most mitigation possibilities. A review of the Heartland Payment Systems data breach also shows me that not only is there a need to protect virtual assets, compromise of physical asset can also pose information leak as the organization did not adequately protect data on its physical device which got compromised. Data encryption was the easy mitigation to that.

Our final project, I was already comfortable enough with these tools to provide first level scanning and tests with most of the easily usable tools. I provided the response to my colleagues via google drive on this link https://docs.google.com/document/d/1EM6rPCpEVg8dZDjrVEqpS6sC44dwPAYEbzTCr_ZGUxI/edit . It was quite easy for us to share our results over this means and we generally discussed on the approach to present our findings. The tutor provided great insight into our draft citing inadequate reference to GDPR and disconnect from our initial draft. We discussed and adjusted our draft based on this feedback. It was a great teamwork, and every single person pulled their weight. I will love to work with these guys once more.

We had a debate on future trends and how CCN, P2P and Mobile all have the potential of being the future of internet. I still do not fully grasp how CCN works and would be my take-home assignment from this module. Although, from the group’s presentation, I will liken it to how Azure works, and services are loaded from your nearest infrastructure… we argued for P2P and since this is the more popular opinion, we had no issue defending our position. I also supported mobile being readily available with the rise of microchip and nanotechnology. However, none of this option might be going away anytime soon and their relationship my be more of complementing rather than competitive.

Overall, this was quite an interesting module for me, I easily followed through all sessions unlike SSDCS where I had a tough time fitting in. The flow of the lecture arrangement was with ease at start and picks up complexity as we go but every module provides a foundation to the next. My biggest take-away from the course is that the cost of mitigation is never as expensive as the implication of the risk.
